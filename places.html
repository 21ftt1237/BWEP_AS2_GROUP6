Deer Farm

Get up close and personal with herds of friendly deers at L&S Deer Farm in Lamunin. A day spent with these gentle creatures will be fun and educational for both adults and children.

Lamin Warisan

Once the home of former penghulu mukim Bendahari Haji Ghafar, this vintage spot is now a museum purposed to preserve Tutong's culture and ethnic records.

Seri Kenangan Beach

If you like long walks on the beach, there is no place better than Pantai Seri Kenangan. It sits beside a long spit of land just 5 minutes away from Tutong Town, with the Tutong River on one side, and the South China Sea on the other.

Tasbee Meliponiculture Farm

Buzz down to Tasbee Meliponiculture Farm where over a 100 nests of stingless bees are kept for their delicious "Kelulut Honey". Apart from their sweet gold, you will find honey soaps, candles and other honey-based products, all locally made from the home-grown bee colonies.

Tasek Merimbun

Tasek Merimbun is the largest natural lake all of Brunei Darussalam, and is designated as one of ASEAN's Heritage Parks. Due to the release of tannin from falling leaves, it's waters glisten with a dark sheen.

Belait

Anduki Recreational Park

Opened in 1992 in commemoration of the silver jubilee of Sultan Haji Hassanal Bolkiah, the Anduki Recreational Park serves as the perfect getaway for the public and tourists alike. Set upon a wonderous view of a glittering skyblue lake, the park offers a peaceful place for an afternoon gathering, or excite your day with some radio-controlled boat racing.

Billionth Barrel Monument

Built in 1991, the Billionth Barrel Monument resides along the beach in Seria and is a symbol of achievement in Brunei’s oil production. Commemorating the accomplishment in its name, the iconic structure stands tall and proud for all to see. This landmark is situated on the beach in Seria that boasts of beautiful sunsets and glimpses of oil rigs in the distant horizon.

Labi Coffee Farm

The Labi Coffee Farm is home to more than 9 hectares worth of coffee, and it is open to visitors! With 18,000 homegrown coffee trees in the expanse of its orchard, it's a grand sight to see. Give your next cup of coffee a Bruneian kick by bringing back some locally grown Arabica and Robusta beans!

Luagan Lalak Recreation Park

Find tranquility in The Luagan Lalak Recreation Park. The park is home to an alluvial freshwater swamp known for its mesmerising beauty. The walkways extend out into the center of the swamp, as the mirror-like surface of the water reflects the open sky with all of its majesty.

Menara Cendera Kenangan

A towering beacon serving as a historic gift commemorating His Majesty's 50th birthday, the Menara Cendera Kenangan has some of the most scenic views in the Belait District.

Mendaram Longhouses

The 12-door, iconic red roofs of Mendaram Besar Longhouse was first built in the 1970s and is now home to about 100 residents. Back then, the house was built with palm leaves.

Temburong

Bangar Town

Unaffected by the fast-paced routine of city life, Bangar Town is a pleasant pause on the ever-moving tale of our life. A thrilling 45-minute speedboat journey away, Bangar is hidden between thick mangrove forest and its lush greenery of nipa palms. The main draw of this serene town is the Ulu Temburong National Park, where you can explore some of the world’s old skyscrapers in the form of centuries-old botanical towers.

Belalong Canopy Walkway

The Belalong Canopy Walkway is considered to by many to be one of Brunei's most majestic locations. Walk across five connected towers with a canopy height of over 43 meters. From up here you can experience a birds eye view of the pristine beauty of Brunei's rainforests, one of the most diverse ecosystems in the world.

Bukit Patoi Recreational Park

Located within the protected Peradayan Forest Reserve, the park are carved with well-marked trails that lead you to peaks that hold breathtaking views of the land below. Make sure you bring lots of water!

Freme Lodge & Adventure Park

Situated on the banks of the great Temburong River, experience the wonders of the rainforest in full magnitude. Dare to cross a suspension bridge to soak in the beauty that surrounds you..

Sultan Haji Omar 'Ali Saifuddien Bridge

Stretching at a remarkable 30km long, Sultan Haji Omar 'Ali Saifuddien Bridge becoming one of the longest bridges in Southeast Asia. The dual-carriageway bridge was opened in March 2020, with the purpose of connecting the Temburong district to the mainland. The cross-district trip brings travellers on a picturesque ride so if you're in the mood to catch sunsets, take a drive on this new bridge.

Sumbiling Eco Village

Lounge lazily in a hammock as the sounds of a thriving rainforest fill the still air. A jungle camp with basic amenities at a riverside location, the Sumbiling Eco Village entails delicious Iban cuisine, exciting outdoor activities like inner tubing and night/dawn walks that allows you to connect with nature.
